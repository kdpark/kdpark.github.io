Gamification-based Social Learning App

Our goal is to bring about a shift of paradigm in digital game design
by creating new user experiences that motivate children to be more curious
about our nature and surroundings in daily contexts.

OUR SERVICES

We have been investigating the design and development of a mobile social learning application leveraging the affordances of social networks and gamification. The design of our mobile social learning application highlights three core design elements:

Location-Based Service

LBS is a social mechanism enabling individuals to be connected in the physical world as well as in the virtual world through their photos.

Gamification

What makes games as a powerful learning tool is the use of game elements, which is called gamification, to promote learner’s engagement and motivation.

Learning Analytics

It is important to analyze and predict activity patterns of users in order to provide more personalized experiences and meaningful content.

OUR FEATURES

Children can use a mobile phone to take digital images of objects that triggered their curiosity or unknown objects wishing to identify. Then, such images are shared in a social platform for knowledge sharing and building via a mobile wireless connection. In our application, there are two main functions (Solve mission and Question Pins) and two sub-functions (POA News and Me).

Let's solve Mission

The main goal of this function is to help users to learn about diverse themes in their surroundings through the activities of solving missions. The mobile system offers a wide range of location-based learning missions that leads users to perform diverse activities based on their interest and context. The system embeds two mechanisms of missions: location-related missions and mission generator. First, location-related missions mainly refer to the type of missions tapping on information in topological spaces, which are further classified to location-specific or location-general missions depending on the level of dependency on physical locations.

  1. Location-specific mission: Children can complete missions when there are located in a particular place. For the initial development, we have selected 20 science museums in Korea and have designed over 200 missions leading users to interact with exhibits, artifacts, resources and activities available in specific locations.
  2. Theme-specific mission: Children can complete missions at any location as long as they are related to a specific theme or phenomenon required in a given mission. Thus far, we have designed over 100 thematic missions.
Missions are also further classified into system-generated or user-generated missions depending on who generate missions. System-generated missions refer to the type of missions that the system provides to users, whereas user-generated missions mean the type of missions that users create for certain locations, activities or themes. Since our design aims to promote users’ intrinsic motivation for participation as a bottom-up mechanism of gamification, we have included the function enabling users to create missions for themselves or other users. We expect that the function for user-generated missions can provide personalized play experiences and promote social engagement.

Question Pins

In the ‘Question Pins’ function, children using a mobile device can take and share digital images reflecting their curiosity and interest about our surrounding (e.g., nature, scientific phenomenon, etc.), and a community of users collaborates to identify and explain unknown species, objects or phenomenon, leveraging the power of collective intelligence in social media. We have designed a new posting and commenting interface called a pin-based annotation system, which encourages users to mark a pin on a photo to describe metadata and to elaborate discussion around photos with comments. The annotative pin system provides users with additional features for entering textual tags and pins in a 2-dimensional image space. The visualized annotators and comments would be improved to evoke active discussion and learning. We expect that the main feature of the ‘Question Pins’ – pin-based annotation – would complement several weaknesses of the current commenting system in social media. First, the current commenting systems that hierarchically accumulate comments may cause problems in understanding as the number of comments increases. Amount of comments beyond human’s capability of instantaneous information acquisition make it difficult to elaborate discussions, where users face difficulties in grasping discussion points in accumulated comments. These phenomena directly contradict to the inherent nature of crowdsourcing that leverages collective features as a powerful mechanism for collective intelligence. Furthermore, in most cases of posts with photos, while the essence of discussion is around photos, the dimension discrepancy between text (1-dimension) and image (2-dimension) may cause troubles in annotating specific positions or areas in photos in the text-only comments interface.

POA News

When users access our application, they enter the page called ‘POA News’ where users can communicate with other users by subscribing activity logs. Furthermore, the system recommends various content based on individual activity data. To design interface reflecting users’ experience in the page, we analyzed several features of commercial social services related to entertainment, collective intelligence and location-based features. From the analysis of the existing platforms, we designed the UI page maximizing social interaction through featuring important social events, timely issues and hot topics (e. g., missions relating scientific themes associated with earthquakes when the fatal earthquake happened in Nepal) and activity logs of followers. Additionally, the POA News page provides users with the updated logs of users’ own postings or activities.

Me

‘ME’ is a space where individual users’ information is displayed. On the page, each user can adjust personal information and customize own status. The page exhibits achievements of gamification elements such as point, level, and badges. The page also provides a space where users can scrap digital images posted by other users. In this page, we expect users to be motivated by this social curation of creating, sharing and (re)mixing digital content.
